The problem

Most traders learn by losing money.

New traders usually enter the market with theory, social media opinions, or borrowed conviction. What they often lack is structured practice. Without enough repetitions, they react to volatility instead of reading it. They overtrade after losses, hesitate on valid setups, chase momentum too late, or hold positions without a plan.

Curious Trader is built to reduce that gap. It gives traders a way to practice decisions repeatedly on historical market scenarios before those decisions become expensive.

Onboarding

Guided onboarding beyond the first login

Every new signup enters a structured 10-step onboarding journey delivered over the first 10 days in the user's preferred language. The goal is not just to explain buttons. The journey helps users understand how to use different modules, how to identify which trading instruments suit them, and how to recognize whether they think better in end-of-day, overnight, or volatility-driven setups. This makes Curious Trader more than a simulator. It becomes a guided practice system.

FAQ

Common questions

What is Curious Trader?

⌄

A suite of mobile trading simulators that lets users practice on historical market data with virtual money.

Is this paper trading?

⌄

It goes beyond basic paper trading by letting users train on past scenarios, use replay-style practice, and work through specific market conditions deliberately.

Who is it for?

⌄

Beginner traders, finance students, interns, and early-stage retail traders who want more practice before using real capital.

Which markets does it cover?

⌄

NSE, FOREX, and CRYPTO.

Do I need real money to use it?

⌄

No. The simulator is designed around virtual-money practice.

Why use historical data?

⌄

Because repetition on past market scenarios helps users practice timing, discipline, and decision-making without live-market pressure.
